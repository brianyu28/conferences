Polls, Graphs and Tables: Incorporating Data into News Incorporating data analysis into reporting has risen to the forefront of modern journalistic practices. Led by one of The Crimson's polling experts, this seminar will review some of The Crimson's largest survey efforts as well as other initiatives to provide readers with real data.
Breaking News: Serving the Public during Times of Crisis In this seminar, students will review The Crimson's coverage of the Boston Marathon Bombing, campus shooting, and bomb threat. Newspapers are perhaps never more important than in these times of crisis, and Crimson editors will share their experiences while also discussing how to prioritize coverage during these stressful times.
How to Launch an Initiative: A Case Study of The Crimson’s Most Recent Blog Since its founding, The Crimson has launched many initiatives to expand its presence and content in the ever-changing field of journalism. The Crimson launched the Flyby blog in 2009, which has continued to provide another outlet for more loosely written, but still well-reported and more opinionated content. This seminar will focus on The Crimson’s most recent initiative to launch a blog on College Admissions and use that project as a case study for how to create and successfully launch your own initiatives.
Keeping Things Light: Online-Only Content on Your Newspaper's Blog Learn from editors of The Crimson's Flyby blog, a daily staple of thecrimson.com that brings thousands of hits to our website daily. Providing a light, tasteful, yet useful perspective on the news, Flyby marks a fantastic example of the next step in online journalism as you bring your high school newspaper into the digital age.
Forming the Feature: A Look at Long-­Form Journalism Some news can be covered in 500 words or less, but other topics warrant extensive in­-depth investigation and a longer story. This seminar will cover several aspects of writing a news feature from how to find a topic, to the research and reporting process, and finally, to structuring and editing the final product.
Inspiring Your Staff: Keeping up Motivation at Student Newspapers Many student newspapers struggle to keep a motivated and dedicated staff as students become bogged down by schoolwork, sports, clubs, college applications, and everything else on their plate. This seminar will explore how leaders at The Crimson keep writers and editors inspired and happy despite all of the pressures associated with being a student.
Column Writing: Making Your Point Knowing how to form a solid argument is an important and versatile skill. In this seminar, students will learn how to craft an opinion on a controversial issue, including how to draft a staff editorial that reflects the view of the entire paper. Crimson opinion editors will also teach the basics of column­ writing, including how to find a topic and how to establish a unique tone and perspective for a column.
Beyond A1: Features Design From the weekly magazine to the annual Year in Review issue to arts and sports supplements, The Crimson’s content offers plenty of opportunities for creative design ideas outside of the daily paper. This seminar will challenge students to examine a number of prominent (and even award-­winning) feature designs from The Crimson’s archives for what makes a design work and what doesn’t.
Graduation 2014: Making a Year in Review Issue The commencement issue is often the biggest single issue that any school newspaper ever publishes--and as such, often the most challenging to produce. In this seminar, discuss The Crimson's own commencement issue and the associated year in review in the context of your own newspaper's annual year-ending issue.
The Making of a Magazine: Alternative Ways To Tell a Story In this seminar, editors of The Crimson’s weekly magazine, Fifteen Minutes, will discuss ways to present campus issues using non-­traditional narrative forms. Students will analyze and critique the effectiveness of various pieces from recent issues of the magazine and brainstorm ways of framing stories for their own papers.
Snapshot of the Game: The Keys to Great Sports Photography Sports photos are often some of the best photos any newspaper publishes. This seminar will explore the best techniques to use in order to capture that perfect action shot or victory embrace.
Telling Stories That Move Audiences: New Medium of Journalistic Videos A well­-made video can tell an engaging story on its own or can serve as a valuable complement to a traditional article. In this seminar, participants will learn the basics of creating a video and discuss how to incorporate more innovative multimedia components into their newspapers’ coverage.
The Art of Covering Faculty Issues Student newspapers often struggle to move beyond coverage of student issues -- simply because those are the issues of which editors and writers are most aware. This seminar will detail the best strategies to covering other campus issues more specific to faculty and staff, but nevertheless important to a newspaper's coverage.
What the Critics Are Saying: How To Write an Arts Review Students will practice how to formulate arguments about an artistic creation and provide support for their claims with concrete points by sampling various clips from plays, movies, and albums as a group. Students will learn how to synthesize their own opinion into a coherent, well­-written review.
Beyond the Game: The Investigative Sports Feature There’s a certain thrill to covering an athletic event, but the world of sports journalism extends far beyond individual wins and losses. What goes into the making of a winning team, or the training of a successful athlete? This seminar will draw on recent feature stories on Harvard sports teams, including the Harvard men’s basketball team, as examples.
Newspaper 2.0: Online Strategy Newspapers’ main sources of readership have shifted online, and drawing more users to their websites is a never­ending quest. This seminar will introduce a number of features of The Crimson’s own online strategy, including web page analytics, online advertising, and The Crimson’s recent website redesign, and its newest initiative for multimedia features.
The Next Generation: How to Train Rising Reporters One of the most important functions of any newspaper is continuing the legacy of strong journalism after the current board of editors departs the organization. As such, few things take higher priority than training up-and-coming reporters. The Crimson's Comp Directors share their insights on how best to foster writing skills, interviewing skills, and of course, paper pride.
Hitting Home with the Readers: Sports Column Writing Crimson sports columnists will share their experience writing a weekly column on one of Harvard's varsity sports teams. This seminar will teach students the fundamentals of sports analysis, as well as the basics of writing a column.
The Future of the Newspaper as a Business: Moving Away from Print Ads and Subscriptions There are many who say the print newspaper as we know it dead already, and publications are quickly evolving to the changing times. In fact, The Crimson itself has already made a dramatic push towards alternative revenue streams than simply subscriptions and print advertisements. How can publications adjust to ensure that journalism remains a profitable endeavor? And what kind of impact are these trends having on college, and even high school, newspapers?
Journalism in the 21st Century: How to Harness the Power of Social Media The work of journalists is being rapidly transformed by presence of social media. What exactly does it mean to be a journalist in the 21st century? This seminar will discuss ways in which newspapers use social media sites such as Facebook and Twitter as a tool for research and reporting, and a platform from which journalists can reach a broader audience. Crimson reporters will discuss their own experience with creating a Twitter presence.
Field Reporting Exercise Students will have an opportunity to practice their reporting skills by interviewing students, tourists, and locals on campus and in the surrounding area. Students will engage with real ­life issues that students and Cambridge citizens face while gaining valuable hands-­on training by reporting in the field.
Photography Excursion In this activity, students will venture out into campus as a group with one of The Crimson’s photographers to capture their own shots of historic Harvard buildings in the square, scenic views on the Charles River, and colorful city life in the Square.
Mock Editorial Meeting The staff editorial is a key part of The Crimson’s Ed Board, and our Editorial Chairs will lead students through a mock editorial meeting. Students will be presented with a set of common controversial school issues up for discussion, and through the course of the meeting, students will debate both sides of each topic and formulate nuanced arguments for potential staff editorials.
Sensitive Stories: The Ethics of Journalism In this discussion, students will engage in a number of key questions relating to journalistic ethics, including what constitutes libel, anonymous sourcing, and how to cover sensitive issues such as sexual assault, discipline cases, and mental health. As these stories have risen to the forefront of many schools' best reporting, their importance cannot be underplayed.

Sell, Sell, Sell: Make That Ad Deal Star-sellers from The Crimson business board will lead this discussion group in which students will have the chance to practice their ad selling skills, perfecting their ability to negotiate, bargain, and close.
